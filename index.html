Before you go

Know before you go

Timing & events

Your week lands just before Salento's festival peak — so it's calmer, but you'll miss the marquee nights:

  • La Notte della Taranta concertone — Aug 22, Melpignano.
  • Lecce's Panorama festival & the Sant'Oronzo feast — mid-Aug.
  • In your week you'll still catch town pizzica nights, summer sagre, and Lecce's open-air programming.
  • Want the big events? Nudging the trip ~10 days later unlocks them.

Pacing for two

Salento is gentler on the feet than the hill towns — and the headline experience is a seated boat ride.

  • Lecce's centre is flat and walkable; the cave boats are calm and shaded.
  • Otranto's old town has a few lanes and steps; the Grotta della Poesia has rocks to the water (the overlook is easy).
  • Your resort spa is the restful anchor between outings. Linen, a hat, and comfortable shoes.

Booking notes

A few things worth locking early:

  • Into Bari, out of Brindisi — one region, no second flight, no backtracking.
  • Book the Aug 7 return as Brindisi → Rome → Miami on one ITA ticket so the connection is protected.
  • Private transfers: Bari→masseria, Valle d'Itria→Lecce (~1.5 hr), Lecce→Brindisi.
  • Reserve the Otranto cave-boat tour and dinners ahead — August fills up.

Good to know

  • Italy uses Type F/L plugs, 230V — bring adapters.
  • Carry some cash; smaller town spots can be card-shy.
  • Driving is optional — with private transfers and a walkable Lecce base you don't need a rental car.
  • Book the cave boat for a morning slot — calmer seas, cooler decks.
